How to Read This Room

This room presents two histories that unfolded on the same land but within different educational systems. One side traces the rise and decline of Roger Williams University, a Black institution founded during Reconstruction to educate teachers, ministers, and civic leaders. The other follows the development of Peabody College, a white teacher-training institution shaped by Northern philanthropy and public investment.

These stories are presented side by side because they were never separate in time or place. They were shaped by the same city, the same state and federal policies, and the same legal boundaries of segregation. Yet access to land, funding, and institutional security differed sharply.

Throughout the room, QR codes offer deeper context—student voices, original documents, and moments of conflict and resilience that cannot fit on the walls. Each QR panel stands on its own and can be explored in any order. Visitors are invited to move back and forth between the exhibits, using these digital panels to explore how education, race, law, and power shaped Nashville’s educational landscape.
