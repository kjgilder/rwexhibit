Relocation and Growth

Roger Williams University underwent a period of significant physical and academic expansion following its relocation to a 30-acre campus on Hillsboro Pike.

Campus Development

During this time, the university developed its infrastructure by expanding the Mansion House for student housing in 1875 and constructing Centennial Hall in 1876, a three-story academic facility funded by New York philanthropists.

Institutional Milestones

This growth was supported by the sale of its original property at Park and Polk Streets in 1876, which subsequently became the Knowles Street School. Academically, the institution reached a major milestone in 1877 when it awarded its first Bachelor of Arts (A.B.) degrees, professionalizing the training of Black teachers and ministers across the South.

A Broader Educational Movement

This era of progress occurred within a broader local educational boom, which included the founding of Meharry Medical College in 1876 and the establishment of the Peabody Normal School in 1875.
